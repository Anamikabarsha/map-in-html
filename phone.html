A telephone is a telecommunications device that permits two or more users to conduct
a conversation when they are too far apart to be heard directly. A telephone converts sound,
typically and most efficiently the human voice, into electronic signals that are transmitted
via cables and other communication channels to another telephone which reproduces the sound to the
receiving user. The term is derived from Greek: τῆλε (tēle, far) and φωνή (phōnē, voice),
together meaning distant voice.
